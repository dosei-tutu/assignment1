Bodegas vs. Supermarkets

Food Insecurity in the Bronx

Bronx, NY, October 7th 2021

The Bronx is known for having food deserts and being one of the most food insecure areas in not only NYC but also the world. The pandemic certainly did not help things as many fellow Bronx residents lost their jobs. According to Feed America, they project that food insecurity in the Bronx will rise to 40%. Most people in the Bronx rely on their delis and bodegas which have limited healthy options in comparison to a supermarket. A lot of supermarkets are unfortunately not in reasonable commutable distances and are not always affordable. There is a citywide initiative to try and stock bodegas and delis with fresh produce and healthier food options for a population that does not always have access to those things. Places such as Hunts Point which has 20 bodegas to one supermarket would benefit from that.

The South Bronx has the highest rate of food insecurity in the country 37% compared to 16.6% in NYC as a whole.

A report by Feed America projects that food insecurity in the Bronx will rise to more than 40% as a result of the coronavirus.

14% of the Bronx’s population suffers from diabetes or obesity, while 30% live in poverty.

1 in 5 children in the Bronx and Brooklyn experienced food insecurity, in comparison to about 1 in 7 children in Manhattan, Queens, and Staten Island.
